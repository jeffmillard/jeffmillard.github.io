Prediction of Coronary Heart Disease

A Shiny Application

Jeff Millard
Coursera "Building Data Products"

Introduction

  • Coronary heart disease (CHD) is the leading cause of death in the United States for men and women.

  • There are uncontrollable risk factors for CHD, such as age and gender, but there are several controllable risk factors, including cholesterol levels, blood pressure, diabetes and smoking.

  • This shiny app shows predictions of the risk of developing CHD over a 10-year period, and shows the contribution of controllable risk factors to the uncontrollable risk factors.

  • The app uses simple inuts, such as age, gender, Total cholesterol (TC), LDL cholesterol (LDLC), HDL cholesterol (HDLC), diastolic and systolic blood pressure (BP), smoking status and diabetes status.

The app does an excellent jod of predicting CHD risk. Try it out, it is very informative!

Predictive Model

The model was published in the American Heart Association journal Circulation.

Prediction of Coronary Heart Disease Using Risk Factor Categories. Peter W. F. Wilson, MD; Ralph B. D’Agostino, PhD; Daniel Levy, MD; Albert M. Belanger, BS; Halit Silbershatz, PhD; William B. Kannel, MD. Circulation. 1998; 97: 1837-1847 doi: 10.1161/01.CIR.97.18.1837


The model was created based on the results of a prospective study, where the patients were 2489 men and 2856 women, 30 to 74 years old at baseline, with 12 years of follow-up.

Two models are compared. The first is based on total cholesterol (TC) levels and the second on LDL Cholesterol (HDLC).
